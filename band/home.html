THE DOORS

Robby Krieger played guitar, John Densmore played drums, Ray Manzarek played keyboards, and Jim Morrison sang in the American rock group The Doors, which was founded in 1965 in Los Angeles, California. After meeting at UCLA's film school, Morrison and Manzarek decided to start a band and quickly enlisted Krieger and Densmore. The Doors' 1967 self-titled debut album, which included the popular song "Light My Fire," launched them to stardom. The band, which combined rock, blues, and psychedelic elements in their music, gained fame in the late 1960s for Morrison's creative lyrics, captivating stage presence, and eccentric demeanor.
